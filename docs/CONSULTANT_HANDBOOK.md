# Consultant Handbook

## Intended Use

Serenova supports a trained consultant during a session. It does not replace
consultant judgment and should not be shown to a client as an autonomous oracle.

## Session Flow

1. Confirm date, time, place, timezone, and how exact the birth time is.
2. Review the birth-time sensitivity section before house or varga claims.
3. Start with natal structure and active dasha.
4. Use transits only as timing context.
5. Review both supporting and attention evidence in each domain.
6. Ask the generated questions before emphasizing a theme.
7. Record usefulness/false-positive feedback after the session.

## Client-Friendly Result

The default result is designed for a reader with no astrology training:

- conclusions and current priorities appear first;
- Sanskrit and calculation mechanics stay inside the expert-details panel;
- English, Hindi, and Hinglish use the same computed facts and ordering;
- changing language does not recalculate or create a second session;
- downloaded summaries retain the uncertainty and professional-advice warning.

## Required Language

Use:

- "may indicate"
- "is traditionally associated with"
- "this period can emphasize"
- "worth discussing"

Avoid:

- guarantees
- exact event claims without corroborating evidence
- fear-based language
- medical diagnoses
- investment, legal, or treatment directives

## Birth-Time Rules

- Exact: house and varga analysis may be used with normal caveats.
- Within 15 minutes: inspect sensitivity changes before using vargas.
- Within 1 hour: treat houses and divisional ascendants cautiously.
- Unknown: do not rely on house-based or varga conclusions without rectification.

## Escalation

Refer clients to qualified professionals for medical, legal, financial, mental
health, safety, or crisis decisions. Astrology must not delay professional care.
